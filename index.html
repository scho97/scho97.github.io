Bio

Welcome to my website! I am a first-year masters student in the OBHA Analysis Group at the University of Oxford advised by Professor Mark Woolrich. In my current research, I study the brain network dynamics of Alzheimer's disease using the Bayesian inference and deep learning methods. I am passionate about understanding complex mechanisms underlying neuropsychiatric disorders by developing and applying computational tools.

I completed my undergraduate study in neuroscience and philosophy at the University of Chicago. My thesis focused on the nonlinear systems of the focal epileptic seizure and was supervised by Professor Wim van Drongelen. After graduation, I worked as a postgraduate research intern in Professor Jee Hyun Choi's lab at the Korea Institute of Science and Technology. I was then an ML/DL research intern in the AutoML team at Lunit.

For full details, please refer to my academic CV.
